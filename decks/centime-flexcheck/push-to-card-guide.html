Push-to-Card
Instant payment to any eligible Visa or Mastercard debit card, settled in seconds
April 2025 Centime Exchange For distribution to clients and suppliers

Centime Push-to-Card (henceforth referred to as Push-to-Card) enables buyers to disburse payment directly to a supplier's Visa or Mastercard debit card in seconds — 24 hours a day, 7 days a week, including weekends and public holidays. Funds are credited instantly to the card's linked bank account with no float period, no ACH delay, and no check to print or deposit.

What it is

Push-to-Card uses the Visa Direct and Mastercard Send rails — the same card network infrastructure used by peer-to-peer payment apps — to push a credit payment directly to the recipient's debit card number. The funds appear in the cardholder's bank account within seconds of the payment being initiated.

The buyer provides only a card number. No routing number, no account number, no bank details. Centime Exchange handles authentication, routing, and settlement.

Why it exists

ACH same-day settlement cuts off at defined windows and does not operate on weekends or holidays. Wires are costly and require full bank account details. FlexCheck requires the supplier to print and physically deposit an instrument. Push-to-Card fills the gap: it is genuinely instant, always available, and requires the supplier to provide only a card number they already carry.

It is particularly suited to time-sensitive disbursements, emergency payments, and suppliers who prefer not to share full bank account details.

"Funds reach the supplier's bank account in seconds — any time, any day, with nothing to print, deposit, or wait for."

How it works

The process from payment approval to funds availability involves five steps. Steps 3 through 5 are fully automated and complete in under 30 seconds in normal network conditions.

1
Buyer approves payment

The buyer's AP team approves an invoice in Centime Exchange and selects Push-to-Card as the payment method.

2
Supplier provides card details

The supplier provides their Visa or Mastercard debit card number through a secure Centime Exchange link. Card details are tokenised and stored for future payments to the same supplier.

3
Card eligibility verified

Centime Exchange verifies the card is eligible to receive a push credit on the Visa Direct or Mastercard Send network. Most standard debit cards issued by US banks are eligible.

4
Payment initiated

Centime sends a credit push instruction to the card network. The network routes the payment to the issuing bank and credits the cardholder's account.

5
Funds available

The supplier receives a notification and funds are available in their bank account — typically within 30 seconds, and in most cases before the notification email is read.

Push-to-Card compared to ACH

Push-to-Card is one of several payment rails available in Centime Exchange. With an average AP ticket size of approximately $4,000, the majority of routine invoice payments fall well within Push-to-Card's standard limits. Push-to-Card is the preferred rail for any payment — AP invoices, expense reimbursements — under $10,000, a threshold we expect to raise over time. Centime's innovation is to embed this powerful instant-settlement rail directly into the AP approval workflow, rather than treating it as a standalone disbursement tool.

Operational detail

Card eligibility

For domestic payments, Push-to-Card credits are supported on most Visa and Mastercard debit cards issued by US financial institutions. Prepaid cards and credit cards are not eligible. For cross-border payments, cards issued in supported international markets are eligible via the Visa Direct OCT international capability. Centime Exchange performs an eligibility check at the point of card registration and will notify the supplier if their card cannot receive push credits, prompting them to provide an alternative card or select a different payment method.

Transaction limits

Standard per-transaction limits are $10,000 on the Visa Direct rail and $10,000 on the Mastercard Send rail. Higher limits are available for established Centime Exchange clients by arrangement. Daily and monthly aggregate limits apply at the platform level and vary by client tier. Buyers whose AP workflow regularly requires disbursements above standard limits should contact their Centime account manager.

Irrevocability

Push-to-Card payments are irrevocable once the credit instruction has been accepted by the card network. Unlike ACH, there is no return window. Buyers should ensure payment details — card number, amount, and payee — are correct before confirming. Centime Exchange requires a confirmation step for all Push-to-Card disbursements above $1,000. In the event of an erroneous payment, recovery requires the supplier's voluntary cooperation; Centime can facilitate but cannot compel return of funds.

Card tokenisation and storage

Supplier card details are tokenised at the point of entry using PCI-DSS Level 1 certified processes. Raw card numbers are never stored by Centime. Once a card is registered, subsequent payments to the same supplier require only the token — the supplier does not need to re-enter their card details. Suppliers can update or replace their card at any time from the Centime Exchange supplier portal.

Source of funds

The buyer can fund a Push-to-Card disbursement from any linked bank account or debit card. Centime Exchange supports multiple funding sources per client, configured at the account level. The funding source is selected at the point of payment initiation.

What suppliers need to know

Receiving a Push-to-Card payment is straightforward. The supplier needs only a Visa or Mastercard debit card linked to their business or personal bank account. No new accounts, no software, and no waiting for business hours.

What you need

  • A Visa or Mastercard debit card (US-issued)
  • An email address on file with your buyer
  • Internet access to register your card once
  • Nothing further — subsequent payments are automatic

What you do not need

  • A new bank account or payment network membership
  • Your routing number or account number
  • Any software installation
  • To be available during business hours
How funds appear in your account
  • Funds are credited to the bank account linked to your debit card
  • The payment will typically appear as a credit from "Centime Exchange" or your buyer's name
  • Most card-issuing banks make push credits available immediately; some may hold funds for up to 30 minutes
  • You will receive an email notification from Centime Exchange when the payment is initiated

Frequently asked questions

Is Push-to-Card the same as a wire transfer?

No. A wire transfer moves funds between bank accounts using routing and account numbers. Fedwire transfers settle in minutes; international SWIFT wires can take hours to days. Push-to-Card sends a credit instruction directly to a debit card via the Visa Direct or Mastercard Send network. It settles in seconds, operates 24/7, and requires only a card number rather than full bank account details.

What happens if the payment is sent to the wrong card?

Push-to-Card payments are irrevocable once accepted by the card network. If a payment is sent to an incorrect card number, Centime Exchange will attempt to contact the receiving cardholder's issuing bank to request a voluntary return of funds. This process is not guaranteed to succeed and may take several business days. Buyers are strongly advised to confirm card details with the supplier before initiating the first payment. Centime Exchange requires explicit confirmation for all payments above $1,000 as an additional safeguard.

Can a supplier use a business debit card rather than a personal one?

Yes — and in most B2B contexts, a business debit card is preferred. Business Visa and Mastercard debit cards issued by US banks are eligible for push credits in the same way personal debit cards are. The funds credit to the bank account linked to the card, which in the case of a business card is the company's business checking account.

Is Push-to-Card available for cross-border payments?

Push-to-Card within the United States operates on domestic Visa Direct and Mastercard Send rails. Cross-border Push-to-Card — sending to a card issued outside the US — uses the Visa Direct OCT (Original Credit Transaction) international capability, which covers over 190 markets. International push payments may be subject to currency conversion and are available for eligible countries; contact your Centime account manager for specific market availability and limits.

How is the supplier's card data protected?

Card numbers are tokenised immediately at the point of entry through a PCI-DSS Level 1 certified process. Centime Exchange never stores raw card numbers — only the token issued by the card network. This means that even in the event of a data breach, exposed tokens cannot be used to initiate unauthorised payments or to reverse-engineer the underlying card number. Suppliers can revoke or replace their stored card token at any time through the Centime Exchange supplier portal.

What if my debit card is declined or flagged as ineligible?

Centime Exchange checks card eligibility at the point of registration — before any payment is attempted. If a card is ineligible (prepaid, credit, or non-US issued), the supplier is notified immediately and prompted to provide an alternative card or to select a different payment method such as ACH or FlexCheck. If an eligible card is subsequently declined during a specific payment — which can occur due to issuer-side controls — Centime Exchange will notify both the buyer and the supplier and prompt the supplier to resolve the issue with their card issuer or provide an alternative card.

Push-to-Card payments are processed via the Visa Direct and Mastercard Send networks. Network availability, transaction limits, and processing times are subject to network operating rules and may vary. Centime Exchange does not guarantee specific settlement times and is not liable for delays caused by card-issuing banks or network conditions outside its control. Cross-border payment availability varies by country. Buyers and suppliers should ensure compliance with applicable financial regulations for their jurisdiction.
