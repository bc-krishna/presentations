FlexCheck
Digitally delivered, supplier-printed payment instruments for mid-market AP
April 2025 Centime Exchange For distribution to clients and suppliers

Centime FlexCheck (henceforth referred to as FlexCheck) is Centime's digitally delivered, supplier-printed check instrument. It gives buyers the speed and control of electronic payment authorisation while giving suppliers the familiarity and universality of a paper check — without requiring either party to adopt new software, new network memberships, or new bank relationships.

What it is

A FlexCheck is a payment authorisation issued by a buyer through Centime Exchange. The buyer approves the payment — amount, payee, check number — and the system generates a fully specified check document, complete with MICR line, payer details, and authorised signature. That document is delivered to the supplier digitally.

The supplier then prints it on any plain-paper printer, endorses it, and deposits it at their bank exactly as they would any other business check. No special ink, no pre-printed check stock, no new accounts.

Why it exists

Many suppliers — particularly in industries where paper checks remain standard — are reluctant to adopt ACH, virtual cards, or proprietary payment networks. FlexCheck meets them where they are: they receive a check. They deposit a check. Nothing changes on their end.

For buyers, the benefit is equally practical. Payment instructions are issued digitally, approved within Centime's AP workflow, and create an auditable record — with none of the cost or delay of physical check printing and mailing.

"The buyer issues the payment digitally. The supplier receives a check. Both parties get exactly what they expect."

How it works

The process from payment approval to bank deposit involves six steps. Each is designed to require the minimum possible action from the supplier.

1
Buyer approves payment

The buyer's AP team approves an invoice in Centime Exchange, selecting FlexCheck as the payment method.

2
Check data is generated

Centime generates a fully specified check document — MICR line, routing number, account number, check number, amount, payee, payer details, and authorised signature.

3
Supplier is notified

An email notification is sent to the supplier with a secure link to retrieve their check from the Centime Exchange portal.

4
Supplier reviews and accepts

The supplier logs in, reviews the check details, and agrees to a short set of terms — primarily confirming they will print and deposit the check only once.

5
Supplier prints

The supplier downloads and prints the check on plain white paper using any standard printer. Colour is recommended; black and white is accepted by banks.

6
Supplier deposits

The printed check is endorsed and deposited in person, via ATM, or via the supplier's bank mobile app — exactly like any other check.

Operational detail

Security and fraud prevention

Every FlexCheck carries a unique check number tied to the specific payment authorisation in Centime Exchange. Banks' duplicate detection systems will reject any attempt to deposit the same check number twice from the same account. The MICR line is rendered to ANSI X9.27 E-13B standards. The check image incorporates a MICR line security background and a pantograph void watermark. The payer's signature is a stored facsimile authorised at account setup, consistent with industry practice for digitally delivered instruments.

Validity window

Each FlexCheck is valid for 90 days from the issue date. After this period, the check data expires and the underlying payment instruction is returned to the buyer for reissuance. Suppliers are sent a reminder at day 75 if the check has not yet been presented.

Deposit methods

The printed FlexCheck is accepted by all standard deposit channels: bank branch teller, ATM, and mobile remote deposit capture (RDC) via a banking app. Suppliers should photograph the printed check — not the screen displaying the digital file. Mobile deposit apps rely on optical character recognition of the MICR line, which requires the check to be physically printed to produce accurate results.

Geographic scope

FlexCheck is supported for US domestic payments and for cross-border payments to Canada, the United Kingdom, and Singapore, where US dollar check clearing times are sufficiently short to make the instrument commercially practical. FlexCheck is not available for payments to India, Germany, China, or other markets where USD check clearing typically exceeds 30 calendar days.

Key operational parameters
  • 90-day validity window from issue date
  • Single presentment only — duplicate deposits are automatically rejected
  • Print on plain white paper; standard printer; colour recommended
  • Endorsed and deposited exactly like any other business check
  • Supported for: US domestic, Canada, United Kingdom, Singapore
  • Check data expires automatically — no manual voiding required by the buyer

What suppliers need to know

Receiving a FlexCheck requires no software installation, no new bank account, and no membership in any payment network. The only requirements are an email address, a printer, and an existing bank account.

What you need

  • An email address on file with your buyer
  • A printer (any standard inkjet or laser)
  • Plain white paper
  • An existing bank account for deposit
  • Internet access to retrieve the check

What you do not need

  • Special check stock or MICR paper
  • A Centime account or login
  • A new bank account or network membership
  • Any software installation
  • Any action before payment is ready

Frequently asked questions

Is this legal? Can a supplier-printed check actually be deposited?

Yes. Under the Uniform Commercial Code (UCC Article 3), a negotiable instrument — which a check is — becomes a legal instrument at the moment it is printed on paper. The digital file in Centime Exchange is not a check; it is check data. When the supplier prints it, they create the physical instrument, acting as the payer's authorised agent for that purpose. This is the same legal construct used by Deluxe Payment Exchange and other established digitally delivered check services. Banks accept these instruments routinely.

Why has this not been done before? What makes FlexCheck different?

Digitally delivered checks are not new — Deluxe has offered a version since the 2000s. What FlexCheck adds is tight integration with an AP automation and supplier management workflow. Rather than a standalone check-delivery product, FlexCheck is one payment rail inside Centime Exchange, alongside ACH and wire, selectable at the point of payment approval. The operational detail — check number sequencing, positive pay integration, supplier notification, expiry management — is handled by Centime rather than requiring the buyer's AP team to manage it.

Is this the same as an eCheck?

Not exactly. Deluxe's eCheck and similar products are consumer- and SMB-oriented services where the payee chooses a deposit method (print, direct deposit, PayPal). FlexCheck is a B2B instrument designed for mid-market AP workflows, tightly integrated with Centime Exchange's supplier network, invoice data, and approval processes. The underlying legal construct is similar; the product context is different.

What happens if a bank questions the check?

The printed FlexCheck includes a verification reference and contact information for Centime. Banks processing the check can verify its authenticity through standard positive pay enquiry processes or by contacting Centime's support line. Each check carries a unique check number that can be cross-referenced against the payment record in Centime Exchange.

Can a FlexCheck be cancelled after it is sent to the supplier?

Yes, provided the supplier has not yet downloaded and printed the check. The buyer can void the check in Centime Exchange at any time before retrieval. Once the supplier has downloaded the check data, voiding requires contacting Centime support. A printed and endorsed check cannot be cancelled by either party — a stop payment request to the bank is required, as with any paper check.

What does the supplier pay for?

Nothing, unless they choose to have the check professionally printed and mailed to themselves rather than printing it at their office. That optional print-and-mail service is available directly through the Centime Exchange portal at the supplier's cost (approximately $2 per check). Downloading and printing locally is free.

The legal terms governing FlexCheck — including the single-presentment obligation, the supplier's agency for printing, and the 90-day validity window — form part of the Centime Exchange platform terms of service accepted by suppliers on onboarding. Individual per-check terms are displayed and accepted at the point of check retrieval. Buyers should consult their legal counsel regarding any jurisdiction-specific requirements for their supplier relationships.
