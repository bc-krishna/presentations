FlexCheck

Digitally delivered, supplier-printed payment instruments for mid-market AP.
April 2025 · For distribution to clients and suppliers
The Concept

Digital authorisation. Paper deposit.

What It Is

A digitally delivered check

The buyer approves a payment in Centime Exchange. The system generates a fully specified check document — MICR line, payer details, authorised signature. The supplier prints it on any plain-paper printer and deposits it at their bank exactly like any other business check.

Why It Exists

Suppliers want checks. Buyers want digital.

Many suppliers — particularly in industries where paper checks remain standard — are reluctant to adopt ACH, virtual cards, or proprietary networks. FlexCheck meets them where they are. Buyers get digital approval and audit trails with none of the cost or delay of physical check printing and mailing.

The Proposition

The buyer issues the payment digitally. The supplier receives a check. Both parties get exactly what they expect.

Process

Six steps from approval to deposit

Each step requires the minimum possible action from the supplier.

1

Buyer approves payment

AP team approves an invoice in Centime Exchange, selecting FlexCheck as the payment method.

2

Check data generated

Centime generates a fully specified check — MICR line, routing, account, check number, amount, payee, and authorised signature.

3

Supplier notified

Email notification with a secure link to retrieve the check from the Centime Exchange portal.

4

Supplier reviews

Logs in, reviews details, and confirms single-presentment terms.

5

Supplier prints

Downloads and prints on plain white paper. Colour recommended; black and white accepted.

6

Supplier deposits

Endorsed and deposited in person, via ATM, or mobile banking app — exactly like any other check.

Common Questions

Three things people ask first

Legal Basis

Is a supplier-printed check valid?

Yes. Under UCC Article 3, a negotiable instrument becomes legal when printed on paper. The digital file is check data; the supplier creates the instrument by printing it, acting as the payer's authorised agent. Banks accept these routinely — the same construct used by Deluxe Payment Exchange.

Differentiation

How is this different from eCheck?

Deluxe's eCheck is consumer- and SMB-oriented. FlexCheck is a B2B instrument integrated into Centime Exchange's AP workflow — alongside ACH and wire — with check number sequencing, positive pay integration, supplier notification, and expiry management handled by Centime.

Cancellation

Can a FlexCheck be cancelled after it is sent?

Yes, if the supplier has not yet downloaded it — the buyer voids it in Centime Exchange. After download, voiding requires Centime support. A printed and endorsed check requires a stop payment request to the bank, as with any paper check. Supplier cost: free to download and print locally; optional print-and-mail service available at ~$2 per check.
