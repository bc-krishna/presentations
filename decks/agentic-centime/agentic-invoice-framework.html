Towards an Agentic Framework
for Invoice Processing

A framework for AI-driven invoice capture, assessment, and approval — what large language models provide, what Centime provides, and why the combination is harder to replicate than it appears.

Centime Inc. 2025 For internal distribution
Executive Summary

The emergence of large language models and AI agents creates a credible path to automating the first mile of accounts payable — the work of getting raw invoices from an email inbox to a ready-to-post state in the ERP. This paper describes the architecture of such a system, the role LLMs play within it, and the proprietary infrastructure that Centime brings to make the approach reliable, trustworthy, and defensible.

The agent framework is available to anyone. What makes it work at the confidence levels required for financial automation is the data and trust infrastructure it runs on — and that is what Centime owns.

1. The Problem This Solves

The first mile remains manual

Conventional AP automation improves the workflow once an invoice is in the system — routing, approval, coding, payment. But the first mile remains largely manual: someone receives an email, downloads a PDF, uploads it to the AP system, and initiates the process.

For a mid-market company processing hundreds of invoices a month, this is a material cost. More importantly, it is an unstructured, error-prone process that creates the ambiguities downstream automation then has to handle.

An agentic approach begins earlier — monitoring an email inbox, capturing and structuring invoices automatically, looking up suppliers, assessing approval readiness using AI, and surfacing only the exceptions that genuinely need human judgment. The goal is not to remove humans from AP. It is to concentrate human judgment where it adds value — on exceptions, on oversight, on policy — and eliminate it where it does not.

2. What Makes a System Agentic

Five characteristics, precisely defined

The term "agentic" is used loosely in the market. Precisely defined, an agentic system has five characteristics that distinguish it from conventional automation:

  • Goal-directedIt is given a goal, not a set of instructions — it determines the steps required to reach it.
  • Persistent stateIt maintains context across a workflow that may span hours or days, remembering prior actions and their outcomes.
  • Autonomous decision-makingWhen the process reaches a fork, the agent chooses a path without requesting human input.
  • Tool use across system boundariesThe agent calls external systems as instruments — email, ERP, verification APIs — and orchestrates across them.
  • Self-correctionWhen an action does not produce the expected result, the agent tries a different approach.

Invoice processing meets all five criteria when properly architected. The agent monitors for invoices, decides how to handle each one, calls external systems to validate and enrich, and routes exceptions — without a human directing each step.

3. The Architecture

From email inbox to ready-to-post

3.1 Ingestion Layer

Email is the primary intake path. The agent monitors a designated AP email address — ap@company.com or equivalent — and detects incoming invoices as they arrive. This is where the majority of supplier invoices enter the system in practice, and the architecture is designed around this reality.

Centime Exchange, the supplier portal, is a secondary path where it exists — one that produces cleaner, more structured input and therefore reduces the agent's extraction workload. Manual upload through the existing Centime AP interface remains available. The quality of the input path directly determines the automation rate achievable downstream.

3.2 The Agent Processing Loop

Once an invoice is ingested, the agent executes a sequential assessment pipeline. Each step produces a confidence score that accumulates into an overall readiness assessment.

  • Extract and structureOCR for scanned documents, followed by LLM-based field extraction regardless of supplier format variation: vendor name, amount, line items, payment terms, PO reference.
  • Supplier lookupThe extracted vendor name is resolved against the Centime vendor master and the ERP's vendor records using fuzzy matching across name, address, tax ID, email domain, and prior payment history.
  • Predictive PO matchingConfidence-scored rather than binary — a 94% confident match on a recurring vendor proceeds; a 67% match on a new vendor is flagged. This replaces the brittle rules-based matching that generates excessive exceptions.
  • AI assessmentGL coding confidence, duplicate detection, amount reasonableness against vendor history, and fraud signals — new bank account, round amounts, entity name variations, recently registered vendors.

3.3 Human Oversight

The AP person receives a periodic digest summarising invoices ready for batch confirmation alongside flagged exceptions requiring attention. The existing Centime AP interface becomes the control tower rather than the primary work surface — where humans exercise judgment over what the agent could not resolve with sufficient confidence, not where routine work gets done.

4. What the LLM Provides

Specific capabilities, not general intelligence

Large language models contribute specific capabilities within the pipeline that would be difficult or impossible to achieve with traditional rules-based approaches.

Importantly, the LLM is not making final financial decisions. It is producing structured, confidence-scored assessments that either clear an invoice for posting or surface it for human review. Financial decision authority remains with the customer's AP team and the policies they have defined.

5. Commodity vs. Proprietary

What anyone can build, and what they cannot

5.1 Commodity components

The following elements are broadly available and do not represent competitive differentiation. Any well-resourced engineering team could assemble them within months:

  • Email monitoring and inbox management
  • PDF OCR and document processing libraries
  • LLM API access for extraction and classification
  • Confidence scoring frameworks and orchestration patterns
  • Basic ERP API integrations

This is the agent framework. It is necessary but not sufficient.

6. Centime's Moat

Trust architecture, not model intelligence

Supplier lookup only resolves reliably because Centime has already enrolled and verified the supplier. Predictive PO matching only achieves high confidence because Centime has transaction history against which to calibrate. The agent's automation rate reflects the quality of this infrastructure, not the sophistication of the model.

Asset 1

Verified supplier network

A competitor can build the same agent. They cannot build a verified supplier network with entity validation and bank account verification at scale without years of enrollment activity.

Asset 2

Bank partnerships

Banks will not white-label an agent framework from an unknown startup. They extend what Centime already has — an established relationship, compliance posture, and proven track record.

Asset 3

Transaction history

Every invoice processed calibrates the confidence models to a customer's specific vendor patterns. The longer a customer uses Centime, the higher the automation rate — a switching cost built into the product.

Asset 4

Production ERP depth

ERP integrations accumulate edge cases and exceptions that only surface in production. Years of live usage creates reliability that is impossible to shortcut.

Each asset grows in value with usage. More suppliers enrolled means better lookup. More invoices processed means better PO matching confidence. More bank partners means broader distribution. The moat is self-reinforcing over time.

7. An Honest Assessment

A 3–5 year journey to full realisation

Agentic AP at the level described here will not be fully realised immediately. Several honest constraints apply:

Input quality

The automation rate is bounded by input quality. Suppliers who submit outside email — in non-standard formats, without PO references — generate more exceptions. The agentic path improves as supplier data quality improves.

Payment execution trust

Autonomous payment execution will lag autonomous approval. Most mid-market Controllers are not ready to remove human authorisation from payment execution entirely, for audit, fraud, and liability reasons.

System readiness

Reliable agentic workflows across ERP, bank, and supplier systems require those systems to be agent-ready — with stable APIs, consistent data models, and reliable event streams. Not all ERP systems are there yet.

Exception design

The exception queue is where human judgment concentrates. Designing that experience well — so the AP person can assess and resolve exceptions quickly — determines whether the automation feels real or just shifts the burden.

Conclusion

The agent is the interface. The infrastructure is the product.

The agentic approach to invoice processing is technically feasible today, commercially compelling, and architecturally aligned with where Centime is already building. The framework described here is not a research concept — it is a near-term product extension built on infrastructure Centime has already developed.

The distinction that matters competitively is not whether a company can build an agent. It is whether the agent has trustworthy data to work with, reliable systems to act within, and bank-channel distribution to reach mid-market buyers at scale.

The supplier network, bank connectivity, transaction history, and distribution channel are the product. The agent is the interface to all of it.
