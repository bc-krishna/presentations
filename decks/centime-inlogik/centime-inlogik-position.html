The Market Moment

A $5B acquisition just changed everything

Capital One’s acquisition of Brex for $5.15B in January 2026 signals that integrated spend management is now a strategic imperative for commercial banks — not a nice-to-have.

  • Category validation: A top-10 US bank paid $5B+ for a spend management platform. Every mid-market bank must respond or risk losing competitive ground
  • Build-vs-buy signal: Capital One chose to acquire rather than build. For mid-market banks without that budget, partnership is the pragmatic path
  • Ramp is the remaining threat: Trusted by 330+ public companies. Over 4,200 companies switched from Brex to Ramp. Positioning explicitly against banks as slow and fragmented
  • 12–18 month window: Banks are newly aware of the threat. Brex customers face uncertainty. No white-label, bank-distributed alternative exists yet

The Gap

Fintechs offer what banks can’t

Brex, Ramp, and BILL combine corporate cards, expense management, AP automation, and bill pay into unified platforms. Banks cobble together disconnected components.

What fintechs deliver

Unified experience: One login, one dashboard, one set of controls across all spend categories.

AI-driven automation: Auto-coding expenses, policy enforcement at point of swipe, duplicate detection.

Speed to value: Customers live in days, not months. Deep ERP integrations out of the box.

What banks currently offer

Fragmented experience: Multiple logins, no unified spend view. Card portal, treasury system, and AP tools are separate.

Manual processes: Expense reconciliation, approval workflows, payment routing remain largely manual.

No brand ownership: Tools carry third-party branding, not the bank’s. Limited stickiness.

Strategic Fit

Why this combination is unique

Four structural advantages that no competitor can replicate.

White-label first

Unlike Brex or Ramp, both Centime and InLogik are designed to disappear behind the bank’s brand. This is foundational to bank-channel distribution — not a bolt-on.

Both sell through banks

Not a theoretical GTM. Centime has 6 US bank partnerships. InLogik has bank and issuer clients globally. Both teams know how banks buy, implement, and support technology.

Zero overlap

Centime does not offer card management or expense tracking. InLogik does not offer AP or payments. The combination is purely additive — no product conflict.

Complementary geographies

Centime has deep US bank relationships. InLogik has global bank relationships and is building US distribution from NYC. Each accelerates the other’s market entry.

Bank Value Proposition

Why banks should care

The combined platform drives revenue, retains deposits, and creates daily engagement — all under the bank’s brand.

Drive Interchange Revenue

InLogik’s CMP drives card issuance at scale. ExpenseMe drives card adoption by making expense workflows seamless. Centime’s virtual card payments extend interchange to vendor payments. More cards issued, more transactions on cards.

Retain Commercial Deposits

When AP and payments run through the bank’s platform, operating accounts stay. Cash forecasting deepens the advisory relationship. Without it, deposits follow the fintech.

Compete Without Building

Both platforms are white-label. The bank presents the solution as its own — consistent with its brand, integrated into digital banking. Fraction of the cost and time of building internally.

Daily engagement = switching costs

Controllers and CFOs log into the bank’s platform every day to approve invoices, review expenses, and check cash positions. This positions the bank as a strategic partner rather than a commodity service provider.

Head-to-Head

What we say to each threat

vs. Brex (Capital One)

Acquired for $5.15B · Now bank-owned

Being absorbed into Capital One’s ecosystem. Product direction will shift to enterprise. Was never white-label — now it’s a competitor’s asset. We offer banks what Brex never did: own the customer relationship while matching the experience.

vs. Ramp

Independent · Most dangerous long-term

Strongest independent competitor. Marketing explicitly against banks as slow and fragmented. Does not offer white-label. Bypasses banks entirely. No cash forecasting or deep supplier onboarding. For banks, Ramp is the threat; we are the response.

vs. BILL / Divvy

$2.5B Divvy acquisition · Direct sales

Offers AP + corporate cards but distributes directly, not through banks. Card management less mature than InLogik’s 30+ year platform. Bank-channel strategy limited to integration partnerships, not full white-label.

The pattern: Every competitor sells directly to companies, bypassing banks. None offer white-label distribution. None let banks retain the customer relationship. That’s our structural advantage.

Go-to-Market

Phased approach to market

Joint bank-channel distribution. The conversation with banks is simple: you need a complete spend management platform to compete with Ramp and Capital One/Brex. We’ve already built the pieces.

Phase 1 — Map

Product & technical assessment. Integration points, combined UX, data flows, API touchpoints. Define the joint offering.

Phase 2 — Pilot

Select one existing Centime bank partner. Pilot the combined offering. Build a reference case in a live bank environment.

Phase 3 — Formalize

Partnership terms, pricing models, joint GTM materials. Begin co-selling to additional bank prospects.

Phase 4 — Scale

Expand across both networks. Centime opens US mid-market banks for InLogik. InLogik opens international banks for Centime.

Revenue model

Bank pays per-customer or per-transaction. Revenue sharing follows the product boundary. Card interchange is a critical driver — every module drives revenue for the bank, incentivizing promotion of the full platform.
