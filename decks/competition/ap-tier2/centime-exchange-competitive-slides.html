Centime Exchange:
The Supplier Enrollment Advantage

Competitive positioning vs. Tipalti, Stampli & BILL — supplier enrollment, verification, document management, and downstream lifecycle effects.

Internal · February 2026 · Confidential

Context

Why supplier enrollment matters

Centime competes well on AP automation and payment execution. We win when we’re in the deal. But mid-market adoption remains low and the landscape is crowded.

  • Our strategy: stack blocks of differentiation — bank partnerships, ERP-native deployment, platform breadth (AP, AR, expense, cash forecasting)
  • Exchange is the latest block — purpose-built supplier enrollment and management that no competing AP platform offers
  • Three outcomes Exchange drives: electronic payment adoption, working capital optimization, and fraud risk reduction
  • Every competitor treats supplier enrollment as secondary — a form to fill out on the way to the “real” product. None have built it as a managed, outcome-driven process

Competitive Landscape

What they offer for supplier enrollment

Tipalti

$350M+ ARR · Closest competitor

Self-service supplier hub, brandable. Strong global payments. But onboarding is a gateway to payments, not a managed process.

No real-time bank verification. No outreach campaigns. No document auto-parse. No fraud indemnification.

Stampli

$100M+ ARR est. · Direct competitor

Advanced Vendor Management (AVM) is a paid add-on with customizable forms and a vendor portal. Shows they know it matters.

No bank verification at all — trusts vendor-submitted data. No early pay. No auto-parse. AVM is bolt-on, not foundational.

BILL

$1.2B+ ARR · Less direct at mid-market

Massive vendor network. Distribution through accounting firms. Increasingly moving upmarket.

No supplier portal. No onboarding workflow. No verification beyond $1 test payments. No document collection. Nothing.

The pattern is clear: every competitor treats supplier enrollment as secondary. None have invested in making it a managed, outcome-driven process.

What Exchange Delivers

Three outcomes no competitor can match

  • Electronic payment adoption. Suppliers enrolled through Exchange have verified bank accounts and active payment profiles from day one. More enrolled suppliers = more ACH & virtual card instead of checks = more rebate revenue and lower processing costs. Outreach campaigns push enrollment velocity from the ~30% typical of single-email invitations to 80%+
  • Working capital optimization. Exchange-enrolled suppliers with verified payment profiles are immediately eligible for early pay — dynamic discounting and supply chain finance powered by bank partner capital. You can’t offer early pay to suppliers you haven’t verified. Exchange makes the supplier base early-pay-ready at enrollment, not months later
  • Fraud risk reduction. Real-time bank account verification proves ownership through browser-based bank authentication. Not micro-deposits (Tipalti: 1–3 days). Not $1 test payments (BILL). Not vendor self-attestation (Stampli: no verification at all). Every payment to a verified supplier goes to a bank account with proven ownership — the single strongest anti-fraud control in the mid-market AP space

Enrollment Velocity   We Lead

Outreach Campaigns

Enrollment velocity directly correlates to risk reduction and electronic payment adoption. Multi-step campaigns vs. single-email invitations.

Centime Exchange

Configurable multi-step campaigns with automated reminders, escalation paths, and deadline tracking. Buyers control the enrollment experience. Real-time completion tracking. Tiered onboarding by complexity — micro suppliers get minimal form, international suppliers get full compliance.

Downstream Effects

Enrollment quality compounds

Supplier enrollment quality compounds through every step of the AP lifecycle. Problems that seem unrelated to enrollment trace directly back to it.

  • Payment to wrong accountReal-time bank verification proves ownership at enrollment. Any bank detail change triggers re-verification. Competitors: micro-deposits, $1 tests, or trust
  • Supplier won’t complete onboardingMulti-step outreach, tiered complexity (micro suppliers get minimal form), mobile-responsive, automated escalation. Competitors: single invite or buyer data entry
  • Expired insurance / lapsed complianceAuto-parsed COI with expiration badges. Renewal alerts 30/60/90 days. Configurable payment block. Competitors: no auto-parse, manual tracking
  • Duplicate vendor recordsERP vendor record pull at enrollment populates existing data. Verified bank account serves as unique identifier. Competitors: de-dup or nothing
  • Invoice exceptions / PO mismatchBorn-digital invoices matched to POs in portal (planned). Supplier resolves exceptions before submission. Competitors: buyer-side matching only

Head-to-Head

vs. Tipalti — Closest Competitor

Our closest competitor. Strong global payments, NetNow early pay, Detect fraud module. But their supplier onboarding is a gateway, not a managed process.

Where we win

Real-time bank account verification (they use micro-deposits). Outreach campaigns (they send one email). Document auto-parse (they collect, don’t parse). Bank channel distribution (they sell direct only). Mid-market accessibility (they price for enterprise at $150K+ implementations).

Where they lead

Multi-language support (27 languages). Built-in OFAC screening pre-payment. Global payment rails (196 countries, 120 currencies). Market presence ($350M+ ARR). Priced and positioned for enterprise.

Positioning: Exchange is purpose-built for supplier enrollment outcomes. Tipalti’s onboarding is a step in a payment workflow. Different design philosophies.

Head-to-Head

vs. BILL — Widest Gap

A bill-pay platform, not a supplier management platform. The gap is structural.

Where we win

Everything supplier-facing. No portal, no onboarding workflow, no document management, no verification beyond manual checks. No supplier-side transactions. No early pay. No outreach capability. The gap here is total.

Where they lead

Network scale — millions of vendors in the BILL network. SMB distribution through accounting firms. Revenue scale ($1.2B+ ARR). Increasingly moving upmarket but their supplier management is nonexistent.

Positioning: BILL is a payment utility. Exchange is a supplier relationship platform. Different products entirely. We win on everything that touches the supplier.

Head-to-Head

vs. Stampli — AP vs. AP+

Best invoice-processing UX in market. Billy AI is genuinely impressive. AVM add-on shows they know vendor management matters — but it’s bolt-on, not foundational.

Where we win

Bank account verification (they have none). Early pay (they offer none). Document auto-parse (they collect, don’t parse). Supplier-side transactions (invoicing, POs, payment visibility). Outreach campaigns (they do remittance-email invites only).

Where they lead

Invoice AI — Billy AI for coding, routing, duplicate detection is genuinely best-in-class. ERP breadth — wider connector library. AVM add-on is emerging but lacks the depth Exchange offers.

Positioning: Stampli is great at invoice processing. Exchange adds the supplier enrollment and management layer they lack. No bank verification at all is their most critical gap.

Bank Distribution

Why Exchange matters to bank partners

Six bank partners today: M&T Bank, City National, FNBO, and others in development. No competitor distributes through banks — Tipalti, Stampli, and BILL all sell direct.

Fraud Controls

Real-time bank account verification and (when reinstated) fraud indemnification on verified payments. Banks need a fraud prevention story backed by real technology, not checkbox compliance.

Electronic Payment Volume

More enrolled suppliers = more payments flowing through the bank’s rails. Deposit retention, transaction revenue, and commercial client stickiness.

Early Pay via Bank Capital

Dynamic discounting and SCF funded through the bank partnership — not anonymous third-party funders. The bank’s capital stays in the relationship. Exchange makes the supplier base eligible from day one.

Structural advantage: No Tier 2 AP platform distributes through bank channels. This is unique to Centime.
