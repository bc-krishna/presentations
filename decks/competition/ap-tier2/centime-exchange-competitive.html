Centime Exchange: The Supplier Enrollment Advantage

Competitive Analysis
Internal — February 2026
Part I — Strategic Narrative

Context

Centime competes well on AP automation and payment execution against Tipalti, Stampli, and BILL. We win when we're in the deal. But mid-market AP automation adoption remains low, the competitive landscape is crowded, and we are not yet in all the deals we'd like to be in.

Our strategy has been to stack blocks of differentiation — bank partnerships, ERP-native deployment, breadth of platform (AP, AR, expense management, cash forecasting). Exchange is the latest block, and it adds something none of our competitors offer: purpose-built supplier enrollment and management that drives electronic payment adoption, optimizes working capital, and reduces fraud risk.

This document focuses on Exchange — what it does, what competitors don't, and why it matters to customers and bank partners.

What Competitors Offer (and Don't) for Supplier Enrollment

Tipalti

$350M+ ARR — Our closest competitor

Self-service supplier hub, brandable. Strong global payments. But onboarding is a gateway to payments, not a managed process.

No real-time bank verification. No outreach campaigns. No document auto-parse. No fraud indemnification.

Stampli

$100M+ ARR est. — Direct competitor

Advanced Vendor Management (AVM) is a paid add-on with customizable forms and a vendor portal. Shows they know it matters.

No bank verification at all — trusts vendor-submitted data. No early pay. No auto-parse. AVM is bolt-on, not foundational.

BILL

$1.2B+ ARR — Less direct at mid-market

Massive vendor network. Distribution through accounting firms. Increasingly moving upmarket.

No supplier portal. No onboarding workflow. No verification beyond $1 test payments. No document collection. Nothing.

The pattern is clear: every competitor treats supplier enrollment as secondary. A form to fill out on the way to the "real" product. None of them have invested in making it a managed, outcome-driven process.

What Exchange Delivers

Exchange is a supplier enrollment and management platform — available standalone or integrated with Centime's payables solutions. It drives three outcomes that no competing AP platform can deliver:

1. Electronic payment adoption. Suppliers enrolled through Exchange have verified bank accounts and active payment profiles from day one. More enrolled suppliers means more payments move electronically — ACH and virtual card instead of checks — which means more rebate revenue and lower processing costs. Outreach campaigns with automated follow-ups push enrollment velocity from the 30% typical of single-email invitations to 80%+. Competitors send one email and hope.

2. Working capital optimization. Exchange-enrolled suppliers with verified payment profiles are immediately eligible for early pay programs — dynamic discounting and supply chain finance powered by bank partner capital. You can't offer early pay to suppliers you haven't verified. Exchange makes the supplier base early-pay-ready at enrollment, not months later.

3. Fraud risk reduction. Real-time bank account verification proves bank account ownership in real time through browser-based bank authentication. Not micro-deposits (Tipalti: 1-3 days). Not $1 test payments (BILL). Not vendor self-attestation (Stampli: no verification at all). Every payment to a verified supplier goes to a bank account with proven ownership. This is the single strongest anti-fraud control in the mid-market AP space.

The Capabilities Behind These Outcomes

Real-time bank account ownership verification. Supplier authenticates via their own bank login. Cryptographic proof of ownership in seconds. No competitor offers anything comparable.

Configurable outreach campaigns. Multi-step campaigns with automated reminders, escalation paths, and deadline tracking. Buyers control the enrollment experience. Competitors offer a single email invitation.

Document management. Auto-parsed insurance certificates (carrier, policy, limits, expiration dates) with visual badges and renewal alerts. Industry-specific document categories (construction, healthcare, IT/SaaS, food, transport). Core business docs (W-9/W-8, Articles of Incorporation, Certificate of Good Standing). Competitors collect a W-9 at registration and stop.

Tiered onboarding. Micro, domestic, and international tiers with different requirements by supplier complexity. Reduces friction for small vendors, enforces compliance for complex ones.

Mobile-responsive supplier portal. Full application on any device. Critical for micro-supplier completion rates. Stampli is desktop-optimized. BILL has no portal.

The Next Block (Planned): Supplier-Side Invoice Submission

Exchange is evolving from enrollment and verification into a transaction hub. Suppliers will submit born-digital invoices directly through Exchange, matched to POs posted by the buyer via API. Suppliers handle their own exceptions before submission. The buyer's AP team receives clean, structured, pre-matched invoices — no OCR, no capture, no conversion. This deepens the value of every enrolled supplier and extends Exchange further into the payables lifecycle.

Why This Matters to Bank Partners

Centime distributes through six bank partners today (M&T Bank, City National, FNBO, and others). No competitor distributes through banks — Tipalti, Stampli, and BILL all sell direct. Exchange strengthens the bank channel pitch in three specific ways:

Fraud controls banks can stand behind. Real-time bank account verification and (when reinstated) fraud indemnification on verified payments. Banks are cautious about what they put their name on. Verified bank account ownership gives them a fraud prevention story backed by real technology, not checkbox compliance.

Electronic payment volume. More enrolled suppliers means more payments flowing through the bank's rails. That's deposit retention, transaction revenue, and commercial client stickiness.

Early pay powered by bank capital. Dynamic discounting and SCF funded through the bank partnership — not anonymous third-party funders. The bank's capital stays in the relationship. Exchange makes the supplier base eligible from day one.

Fraud Indemnification: In Progress

Centime holds a lapsed Lloyd's Financial Institutions Bond (B1230FC) with zero claims history. We are actively pursuing reinstatement with broadened coverage for social engineering and vendor impersonation fraud. No competing AP automation platform offers any form of fraud indemnification on supplier verification. When active, this becomes the capstone of Exchange's value — for customers and bank partners alike.

With planned supplier-side invoice submission, Exchange extends further into the payables lifecycle. With bank distribution that no competitor has and fraud indemnification that no competitor offers, Exchange doesn't just differentiate Centime's payables platform — it strengthens the entire bank channel proposition.
