Centime Exchange vs. PaymentWorks

Competitive Analysis
Internal — February 2026
Part I — Strategic Narrative

Who They Are

PaymentWorks is a supplier onboarding and identity verification platform, in market since 2013. They serve approximately 200 customers — primarily higher education institutions and public sector organizations — with estimated annual revenue of ~$12M. Their marquee relationship is with J.P. Morgan.

PaymentWorks' primary competitive differentiator is not their software. It is their fraud indemnification — up to $2M per fraudulent payment, bundled into every customer contract. In competitive evaluations, the indemnification is frequently the deciding factor. The software capabilities are table stakes. The insurance is the close.

Who We Are

Centime is a financial process automation company providing mid-market finance teams with AP automation, AR automation, expense management, business banking, and cash flow forecasting — embedded directly within their ERP (NetSuite, Sage Intacct, QuickBooks, Microsoft Dynamics). On the payables side, Centime offers two complementary solutions:

AP Automation (Invoice-to-Pay)

  • AI-powered invoice capture & coding
  • PO matching (2- and 3-way)
  • Approval workflows
  • Supplier management

Payments Automation (Integrated Payables)

  • Payment execution: ACH, virtual card, check
  • ERP reconciliation
  • Transaction monitoring
  • Dynamic discounting & supply chain finance

Centime Exchange is the supplier enrollment and verification layer that serves both payables solutions. Exchange can be deployed standalone — for buyers who have existing AP systems but need verified supplier onboarding — or integrated with Centime's payables solutions, where its value compounds across the full invoice-to-payment lifecycle.

A company called "PaymentWorks" that processes no payments.

PaymentWorks collects supplier data and hands it off. They have no invoice processing, no payment execution, no ERP integration, no reconciliation. The name implies a payables platform. The product is a supplier form. Centime delivers the full lifecycle — from supplier verification through invoice capture, approval, payment, and remittance — that "PaymentWorks" suggests but cannot provide.

Why We Win: Two Arguments

Argument 1: Exchange standalone is already better

Even without Centime's payables solutions, Exchange leads PaymentWorks on most supplier management capabilities. Exchange proves bank account ownership in real time through browser-based bank authentication — not micro-deposits, not third-party database lookups, not self-attestation. Our outreach engine runs configurable campaigns with automated follow-ups and deadline-driven enrollment. Our document management handles core business docs, insurance certificates with automated parsing and expiration tracking, and industry-specific document categories. Our portal is fully mobile-responsive. Theirs is desktop-only. Exchange is fully embeddable with complete API support — it can be embedded into any buyer workflow or third-party system. And our platform supports international suppliers with an I18N-capable interface, allowing overseas suppliers to complete onboarding in their local language.

The customer who only wants supplier verification — because they already have an AP system — gets a better product with Exchange than with PaymentWorks.

Argument 2: With Centime's payables solutions, the gap becomes a chasm

When Exchange is paired with AP Automation and Payments Automation, Centime extends into territory PaymentWorks cannot reach: native ERP integration, PO posting and invoice matching (with supplier-side exception handling), born-digital invoicing that eliminates OCR entirely, payment execution across ACH/virtual card/check, remittance detail back to the supplier portal, and dynamic discounting powered by the buyer's own bank partner — not anonymous third-party funders.

PaymentWorks competes with Exchange standalone. They have no answer for Exchange integrated with Centime's payables solutions. None.

Fraud Indemnification: Their Lead, Our Path

We are direct about this: PaymentWorks leads today on fraud indemnification. Their $2M bundled coverage is a real competitive advantage, particularly with risk-averse procurement committees.

PaymentWorks — Today

Up to $2M per fraudulent payment, bundled into platform fee. Underwritten by specialty insurer. No separate purchase decision. Their primary sales weapon.

Centime — In Progress

Centime holds a lapsed Lloyd's Financial Institutions Bond (B1230FC) with zero claims history. We are actively pursuing reinstatement with broadened coverage for social engineering and vendor impersonation fraud — the fraud vector Exchange verification is specifically designed to prevent. We are evaluating multiple structures including a master facility with per-customer premium.

The underwriting case is strong: Our real-time bank account ownership verification is more rigorous than PaymentWorks' validation methods, we have a zero-claims track record, and our bundled distribution model (every payables customer automatically enrolled) eliminates adverse selection. The gap is timing, not capability.

Where They Win (and Why It Doesn't Matter Much)

Higher-ed and public sector compliance. PaymentWorks has deep penetration in universities and government agencies where compliance-heavy onboarding and fraud indemnification are procurement requirements. We are not targeting this segment. Our distribution is through bank partnerships — six today, including M&T Bank and City National — serving mid-market commercial banking clients. Different buyer, different channel, different economics.

KYB/compliance bundled into platform fee. PaymentWorks includes KYC/KYB screening in their base price; Centime offers it as a per-report option ($25 via Middesk). This is a business model choice, not a capability gap — and our approach is more flexible for mid-market buyers who don't need compliance reports on every supplier.

Early Pay — Fundamentally Different Models

PaymentWorks EarlyPay

0% – 3.0%
Third-party funded SCF only. Anonymous funder pays supplier early; buyer pays funder on due date. 30-day sliding scale — supplier pays 1.0% even at day 20. PaymentWorks doesn't process the payment.

Centime (via Payments Automation)

0% – 2.75%
Dynamic discounting + SCF, powered by bank partner capital. $0 fee from day 20–30. Capital stays in the existing banking relationship. Buyer earns card rebates.

The Bottom Line

Exchange standalone already beats PaymentWorks on most supplier management capabilities — verification, outreach, document management, mobile access. Paired with AP Automation and Payments Automation, Centime extends into the full invoice-to-payment lifecycle — ERP integration, PO matching, payment execution, remittance, early pay — territory that a company called "PaymentWorks" has never entered. Their fraud indemnification leads today; we are actively closing that gap with stronger verification controls and a zero-claims underwriting history. The customer who wants supplier management gets a better product. The customer who wants the full payables lifecycle gets something PaymentWorks cannot compete with at all.
