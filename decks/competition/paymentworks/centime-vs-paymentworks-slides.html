The Competitor

Who is PaymentWorks?

A supplier onboarding and identity verification platform. In market since 2013. Their competitive weapon is insurance, not software.

  • ~200 customers — primarily higher education and public sector
  • ~$12M annual revenue — estimated
  • Marquee relationship: J.P. Morgan
  • Primary differentiator: Up to $2M fraud indemnification per payment, bundled into every contract
  • In competitive evaluations, the indemnification is frequently the deciding factor. The software is table stakes. The insurance is the close.

Our Position

Who is Centime?

Full-lifecycle financial process automation for mid-market finance teams, embedded directly in the ERP.

AP Automation (Invoice-to-Pay)

  • AI-powered invoice capture & coding
  • PO matching (2- and 3-way)
  • Approval workflows
  • Supplier management

Payments Automation (Integrated Payables)

  • Payment execution: ACH, virtual card, check
  • ERP reconciliation
  • Transaction monitoring
  • Dynamic discounting & supply chain finance

Centime Exchange is the supplier verification layer that serves both solutions. Deployable standalone or integrated with the full payables platform.

Argument 1

Exchange standalone is already better

Even without Centime’s payables solutions, Exchange leads PaymentWorks on most supplier management capabilities.

  • CAS proves bank account ownership in real time — not micro-deposits, not database lookups, not self-attestation
  • Outreach engine runs configurable campaigns with automated follow-ups and deadline-driven enrollment
  • Document management handles core business docs, insurance certificates with automated parsing and expiration tracking
  • Fully mobile-responsive portal — theirs is desktop-only

The customer who only wants supplier verification gets a better product with Exchange than with PaymentWorks.

Supplier Verification

Bank Account Verification

We Lead — Strongest anti-fraud differentiator

Centime Exchange

Real-time ownership proof via CAS (Centime Account Services). Supplier logs into their own bank through a secure browser session. We confirm the account number matches an account they control.

Not a micro-deposit. Not a database lookup. Real-time proof of ownership.

PaymentWorks

Third-party validation services, micro-deposits (2–3 day delay), and supplier self-attestation.

Multiple fallback methods, none of which prove the supplier actually controls the account in real time.

Fraud Indemnification

Their lead, our path

We are direct about this: PaymentWorks leads today on fraud indemnification. The gap is timing, not capability.

PaymentWorks — Today

Up to $2M per fraudulent payment, bundled into platform fee. Underwritten by specialty insurer. No separate purchase decision. Their primary sales weapon.

Centime — In Progress

Lapsed Lloyd’s Financial Institutions Bond (B1230FC) with zero claims history. Actively pursuing reinstatement with broadened social engineering and vendor impersonation coverage. CAS provides the underwriting control.

CAS provides real-time bank account ownership verification more rigorous than PaymentWorks’ methods. Our bundled distribution model eliminates adverse selection. The underwriting case is strong.

Early Pay

Fundamentally different models

PaymentWorks EarlyPay

0% – 3.0%

Third-party funded SCF only. Anonymous funder pays supplier early; buyer pays funder on due date. 30-day sliding scale — supplier pays 1.0% even at day 20. PaymentWorks doesn’t process the payment.

Centime (via Payments Automation)

0% – 2.75%

Dynamic discounting + SCF, powered by bank partner capital. $0 fee from day 20–30. Capital stays in the existing banking relationship. Buyer earns card rebates.

Honest Assessment

Where they win

And why it doesn’t matter much for our market.

Higher-ed & public sector compliance

PaymentWorks has deep penetration in universities and government agencies where compliance-heavy onboarding and fraud indemnification are procurement requirements. We are not targeting this segment. Our distribution is through bank partnerships — six today, including M&T Bank and City National — serving mid-market commercial banking clients. Different buyer, different channel, different economics.

KYB/compliance bundled into platform fee

PaymentWorks includes KYC/KYB screening in their base price; Centime offers it as a per-report option ($25 via Middesk). This is a business model choice, not a capability gap — and our approach is more flexible for mid-market buyers who don’t need compliance reports on every supplier.

Distribution

Bank channel distribution

Centime distributes through bank partnerships. Six banks today, including M&T Bank, City National, and First National Bank of Omaha, with additional partnerships in active development.

  • Banks white-label Centime’s payables platform for their commercial banking clients
  • Bank partners are highly receptive to fraud indemnification as a differentiator
  • It strengthens commercial banking relationships and reduces payment fraud risk on their rails
  • No Tier 2 AP platform distributes through bank channels — this is a structural advantage
