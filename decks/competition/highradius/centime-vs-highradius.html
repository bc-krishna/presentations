Part I — Strategic Narrative

Who They Are vs. Who We Are

HighRadius is a $3.1B-valuation, 4,500-employee autonomous finance platform headquartered in Houston. They are known — correctly — as the market leader in accounts receivable automation, where they've held Gartner Magic Quadrant Leader status three consecutive years. Their customer base reads like the Fortune 500: 3M, Unilever, Anheuser-Busch InBev, Sanofi, Hershey's, Danone, Kellogg Company.

Their AP automation product is new. Launched in April 2024 as a “GenAI-native” solution, it extends their platform from O2C and Treasury into the payables lifecycle. The product suite includes invoice processing, supplier management (with a “Supplier Connect” GenAI portal), dynamic discounting, AP reporting, and ERP integration. It is well-engineered, heavily marketed, and backed by the resources of a company with 850+ enterprise customers.

Centime is a full-featured AP Automation and financial process automation platform serving mid-market finance teams through bank partnerships. We are a smaller company — but not a less experienced one. Centime's AP Automation has been in market for several years, and the founding team previously built MineralTree, itself a leading AP automation platform. That means the people designing, building, and delivering Centime's AP solution have been doing this for over a decade. We know where the inefficiencies hide, where implementations break down, and what mid-market finance teams actually need versus what enterprise vendors assume they need. HighRadius launched AP in April 2024. We've been solving these problems since before they started.

This distinction shapes everything: product design, pricing, implementation, distribution, and what each platform optimizes for. Where HighRadius Is Strong

We are honest about what they do well. On several dimensions, HighRadius has advantages that reflect their scale and enterprise focus.

Enterprise invoice volume at scale. HighRadius claims 90%+ touchless invoice processing through “Agentic AI” that evaluates vendor history, PO alignment, approval patterns, and freight thresholds. Their multi-channel intake includes EDI, cXML, and e-invoice network support — enterprise integration patterns that their Fortune 500 customers require. Centime's GenAI-powered capture and coding is fully competitive on the AI front; the difference is channel breadth for enterprise use cases (EDI/cXML), not AI capability.

Dynamic discounting with marketplace negotiation. HighRadius offers a dynamic discounting platform where suppliers propose terms and buyers counteroffer — a true negotiation model, not just a fixed discount schedule. They claim 3x early payment discount capture and 98% discount capture rate. This goes beyond standard early pay programs.

Supplier Connect (GenAI). Their supplier portal uses GenAI for automated supplier communication — instant responses to payment inquiries, sentiment analysis, proactive task generation for exceptions. This reduces the AP team's supplier communication burden at scale.

Analyst recognition. Gartner Magic Quadrant Leader (Invoice-to-Cash, 3x), IDC MarketScape Leader (AR Automation, Embedded Payments), Forrester recognition. This carries weight in enterprise procurement decisions.

Where Centime Wins

Start with the fundamentals: Centime is a full-featured AP Automation solution with everything a mid-market finance team needs to eliminate manual payables work — and it's part of a broader financial process automation suite that HighRadius doesn't offer for this market.

Complete AP Automation. GenAI-powered invoice capture and coding. 2-way and 3-way PO matching. Configurable multi-level approval workflows with role-based routing and threshold rules. Full payment execution — ACH, virtual card, check, and cross-border local currency payments via Visa/Currencycloud. This is not a partial solution or a niche tool — it covers the entire procure-to-pay lifecycle, and the core features are competitive with any platform on the market, including HighRadius.

Built for the mid-market. Every design decision — the ERP integration model, the pricing, the implementation approach, the bank distribution channel — is optimized for mid-market finance teams ($50M–$500M revenue). HighRadius is built for Fortune 500 enterprises with dedicated procurement departments, IT staff, and implementation budgets. These are fundamentally different products for fundamentally different buyers.

Out-of-the-box ERP integration, rapid implementation. AP Automation is fully embedded in the ERP — QuickBooks, Sage Intacct, NetSuite, Microsoft Dynamics — as a native extension. No professional services. No multi-month implementation. Up and running in days, not quarters. For larger enterprises, Integrated Payables offers an ERP-agnostic, payables-only track. HighRadius lists 50+ ERP integrations, but each is a professional services engagement — SOWs, timelines, consulting. That model works for Fortune 500 companies. It does not work for mid-market buyers or for banks distributing AP to their commercial clients.

Scalable pricing. Centime's pricing scales with mid-market budgets — not enterprise procurement cycles. No six-figure implementation fees. No multi-year enterprise contracts required to get started.

Part of a financial process automation suite. Centime isn't just AP. It's AP Automation, Payments Automation, Cash Flow Forecasting, and Exchange — an integrated platform that gives mid-market CFOs and Controllers visibility across payables, receivables, cash position, and supplier relationships. HighRadius offers platform breadth too (O2C, Treasury, R2R, AP), but it's architected for enterprise — not for the mid-market finance team that needs the whole picture without enterprise complexity.

Critical Differentiators

Beyond the core AP platform, Centime has structural advantages that HighRadius cannot replicate — regardless of R&D investment.

Exchange + CAS

Purpose-built supplier enrollment with real-time bank account ownership verification. HighRadius has a supplier portal with onboarding and tax validation — but no equivalent to CAS. They validate bank details against external databases. We prove ownership through browser-based bank authentication. That is a fundamentally different level of fraud prevention.

Bank Distribution

Six bank partners (FNBO, M&T Bank, City National, Integro Bank, and others) white-label Centime's platform for commercial banking clients. HighRadius sells direct to enterprise. They have 110+ bank integrations for payment rails — but no bank distribution channel. Banks don't resell HighRadius. They resell Centime.

FlexPay: Bank-Funded Early Pay

STP with early payment powered by bank partner capital — not the buyer's cash. Suppliers opt in per invoice. 0–2.75% sliding scale. HighRadius dynamic discounting requires buyers to deploy their own surplus capital. FlexPay serves a market segment that approach cannot reach.

FlexPay vs. HighRadius Dynamic Discounting

Both platforms offer early payment capabilities, but the mechanics differ fundamentally.

HighRadius Dynamic Discounting

Marketplace model: suppliers propose discount terms, buyers counteroffer. AI recommends opportunities based on cash surplus. Supports due and past-due invoices. Integrates with Treasury for cash visibility. Strong for enterprises with $100M+ AP spend and significant cash positions.

Centime FlexPay (STP + Early Pay)

Straight-through processing with early payment powered by bank partner capital — not the buyer's cash. Suppliers opt in per invoice via Exchange. 0–2.75% sliding scale. Bank-funded model means mid-market buyers offer early pay without committing working capital. Generates revenue for the bank partner.

The difference: HighRadius dynamic discounting is a cash deployment tool for large enterprises with surplus capital. FlexPay is a bank-funded early payment program that mid-market companies can offer without tying up their own cash.

Virtual Card Support

Both platforms support virtual card payments. HighRadius processes virtual cards within their integrated payables framework — one consolidated payment file supporting ACH, check, and virtual card, routed by supplier preference. They also discuss virtual card rebate programs as a way to turn AP into a revenue-generating function.

Centime's Payments Automation supports virtual card execution alongside ACH and check, integrated with ERP reconciliation. Virtual card adoption is driven by Exchange: when suppliers enroll through Exchange with verified payment profiles, transitioning them to virtual card from check becomes a structured outreach campaign — not a one-off request. This drives higher virtual card adoption rates and more rebate revenue for the buyer.

The Supplier Portal Gap

HighRadius has a supplier portal — and it's not trivial. Supplier Connect provides self-service onboarding, W-9/W-8 collection, bank detail submission, invoice status tracking, and GenAI-powered query resolution. Their claims of 70% faster onboarding and 3x fewer supplier queries reflect real investment in this area.

What they don't have:

CAS — real-time bank account ownership verification. HighRadius validates bank details against external databases and flags discrepancies. That catches formatting errors and known-bad accounts. It does not prove that the person submitting the bank details owns the account. CAS does.

Configurable outreach campaigns. HighRadius onboarding is portal-based: suppliers are invited, they register, data is validated. There's no multi-step outreach engine with automated follow-ups, escalation paths, and deadline tracking. For mid-market companies trying to get 500 suppliers enrolled without a dedicated procurement team, outreach automation is the difference between 30% and 80% completion rates.

Comprehensive document management. HighRadius collects tax forms and compliance certificates at onboarding. They do not auto-parse insurance certificates, track expiration dates with visual badges, offer industry-specific document categories, or provide automated renewal alerts. Exchange does all of this.

Part III — Positioning & Competitive Strategy

When Do We Compete?

HighRadius and Centime rarely compete head-to-head for the same deal. Their target segments are different, their distribution models are different, and their price points are different. But awareness of HighRadius matters in four scenarios:

Scenario 1: The Upmarket Mid-Market Buyer

A $300M–$500M company evaluating AP automation. Large enough to consider enterprise platforms, small enough that HighRadius's implementation overhead is a concern. Our play: Lead with total cost of ownership, implementation speed, and bank partnership. Show that Exchange exceeds HighRadius on supplier enrollment, particularly CAS and outreach automation.

Scenario 2: Bank Partner Conversations

A bank evaluating AP platforms for commercial clients. HighRadius may surface as a “name brand.” Our play: HighRadius doesn't distribute through banks. Their 110+ bank connections are payment rails, not distribution partnerships. Centime is the only platform built for bank partnership from day one.

Scenario 3: The AR-to-AP Cross-Sell

An existing HighRadius AR customer considering their new AP module. Path of least resistance: one vendor, one contract. Our play: This is their strongest position. Lead with Exchange (they don't have it), FlexPay bank-funded early pay, and total cost. If they want best-of-breed AP with verified supplier enrollment, Centime wins.

Scenario 4: Supplier Verification Evaluation

A buyer specifically evaluating supplier verification and fraud prevention. Our play: We win decisively. CAS real-time bank ownership proof, Didit AML/PEP/sanctions screening, TINCheck EIN validation, auto-parsed document management, configurable outreach campaigns. No HighRadius equivalent exists.

The Bottom Line

Centime delivers a full-featured AP Automation solution — GenAI invoice capture and coding, 2-way and 3-way PO matching, configurable approval workflows, and complete payment execution including cross-border via Visa/Currencycloud — embedded in mid-market ERPs, deployed without professional services, and priced for mid-market budgets. On core AP capabilities, we are competitive with HighRadius feature for feature.

Where we separate is on structural advantages HighRadius cannot replicate. Exchange and CAS deliver supplier verification capabilities they don't have — real-time bank account ownership proof, configurable outreach campaigns, auto-parsed document management, and comprehensive AML/PEP screening. FlexPay offers bank-funded early payment without the buyer deploying their own capital. Bank distribution gives us a channel HighRadius cannot access. And fraud indemnification, when reinstated, adds a layer no AP automation platform in the market offers.

The competitive strategy: we lead with a complete AP platform built for how mid-market companies actually buy and deploy software — then win on supplier verification, bank distribution, and working capital innovation where we have structural advantages that enterprise investment cannot easily replicate.
