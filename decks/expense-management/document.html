Centime · Product Specification

Centime Expense Management

A policy-driven expense and lightweight purchasing platform for US mid-market finance teams, built on Findity's white-label T&E engine.
Version 0.3 · Draft · February 2026 · US Market · Internal
01 — Overview

What we are building

Centime Expense Management is a policy-driven expense and lightweight purchasing platform for US mid-market finance teams, built on Findity's white-label T&E engine. We own the UX and workflows; Findity provides the expense processing engine. The first objective is to build a differentiated user experience and workflow layer. The system enforces corporate card usage as the default payment method, provides configurable policy guardrails, and delivers a streamlined experience across three core personas.

Travel & Entertainment

  • The core use case covering meals, travel, lodging, mileage, and incidentals

Basic Purchasing

  • Controlled spend for supplies, software subscriptions, professional services, and other operational purchases requiring pre-approval

Design Principles

Corporate Card First

Every policy default, UX nudge, and workflow assumes corporate card usage via our US Bank (primary) and FNBO (secondary) card programs. The system is card-agnostic -- it accepts any Visa or Mastercard -- but pricing favors our cards: free with committed spend on our programs, per-active-user/month for customers using their own cards.

Policy at the Center

Rules are not bolted on -- they shape the UI the employee sees, the options available, and the approval routing.

Mid-Market Pragmatism

Enough control for a 200-2,000 employee US company without the configuration overhead of enterprise ERP expense modules. Findity's proven engine handles the processing complexity; we focus on UX and workflows.

Accountability Without Friction

Capture what finance needs for audit and GL coding without making employees hate the process.

02 — Personas & Core Needs

Who we are building for

Three core personas define every workflow, screen, and policy configuration in the system.

Employee

Submitter -- road warriors, occasional travelers, department leads making purchases
  • Submit expenses quickly with minimal data entry
  • Understand what's covered by policy before spending
  • Know the status of submitted expenses and pending reimbursements
  • Use corporate card with transactions flowing in automatically

Manager

Approver -- direct managers, department heads, project leads
  • Review and approve/reject expenses efficiently, ideally from mobile
  • See policy violations and out-of-policy flags without reading every line
  • Delegate approval authority when out of office

Finance

Controller / Reimburser -- AP team, finance managers, controllers
  • Enforce policy consistently across the organization
  • Reconcile corporate card statements against submitted expenses
  • Process reimbursements for personal-card expenses
  • Ensure proper GL coding and cost center allocation
  • Maintain audit trail and compliance documentation

Key Frustrations to Solve

Employee

  • "I don't know what I'm allowed to spend"
  • "I lost a receipt and now I'm stuck"
  • "I submitted two weeks ago and have no idea where it is"

Manager

  • "I get a pile of expense reports at month-end"
  • "I can't tell which ones actually need my attention"

Finance

  • "Employees submit late and I can't close the period"
  • "Half the card transactions are unmatched"
  • "I spend hours fixing GL codes"
03 — Policy Engine

The backbone of the system

Policies are not just validation rules -- they shape the employee experience, determine approval routing, and control payment method availability.

Policy Hierarchy

Policies are evaluated in a layered hierarchy. More specific policies override general ones.

Company Default
Base rules that apply to all employees across all departments
Department / Division Override
Department-specific rules override company defaults
Role-Based Override
Executive, Sales, and other role-specific policy adjustments
Project / Event Override
Temporary overrides scoped to a specific project or event

Corporate Card Mandate Levels

These mandate levels apply to corporate cards issued through our US Bank (primary) and FNBO (secondary) card programs. The system is card-agnostic and will accept transactions from any Visa or Mastercard, but policy enforcement and real-time feed availability are optimized for our issued cards.

Level Name Behavior
1 Encouraged Personal card accepted; employee sees a reminder that corporate card is preferred.
2 Preferred Personal card accepted but requires a written justification field. Justification visible to approver and finance.
3 Required Personal card submission blocked. Employee must use corporate card or submit a policy exception request that routes to finance for pre-approval.
4 Mandatory with Auto-Reject Personal card expenses are auto-rejected with a message directing the employee to use their corporate card. No exception path without finance override.

Default recommendation: Level 2 (Preferred) as company default, Level 3 (Required) for T&E categories above $50. Note: card-agnostic acceptance means customers using non-program cards (any Visa/Mastercard) still get full policy enforcement -- they just pay per active user instead of getting the platform free with committed spend.

Spend Policies

Policy Type Configuration Options
Per-Diem Limits Daily caps by expense type (meals, lodging, transport) with city/region tiers
Per-Transaction Limits Max amount per single expense line by category
Monthly/Quarterly Caps Rolling spend limits per employee or department
Category Restrictions Blocked categories (e.g., no alcohol for certain departments)
Receipt Thresholds Dollar amount above which receipt attachment is mandatory
Advance Booking Minimum days in advance for flights/hotels to qualify for standard approval
Preferred Vendor Rules Flag or block expenses at non-preferred vendors (hotel chains, airlines, car rental)
Meal Attendee Requirements Require attendee names and business purpose for meals above a threshold

Policy Violation Handling

  • 01Soft Warning -- Employee sees a yellow flag but can still submit. Approver sees the flag.
  • 02Hard Warning -- Employee must acknowledge the violation and provide justification before submitting. Violation is prominently flagged for approver.
  • 03Block -- Expense cannot be submitted. Employee is directed to the correct action.

Policy Visibility

Employees should never have to guess. The system surfaces applicable policies at three moments.

  • Pre-trip / Pre-purchase: "Your meal per-diem in Chicago is $75/day. Hotel limit is $250/night."
  • At submission: Real-time validation as fields are completed -- green check or warning before they hit submit.
  • In rejection: Clear, specific reason tied to a named policy -- not a generic "rejected by manager."
04 — T&E Expense Management

The core expense workflow

Travel and entertainment is the primary use case. Two submission paths serve different payment methods, with the corporate card path as the default.

Corporate Card Transaction Flow

This is the primary path. Card transactions flow in via real-time Visa and Mastercard network feeds and the employee completes the record. We do not have real-time Amex integration; Amex transactions require manual entry or batch file upload.

  1. Card transaction posted via real-time Visa or Mastercard feed
  2. Auto-matched to merchant category via MCC code; pre-populated expense line created
  3. Employee reviews, adds receipt, confirms GL/project code
  4. Routed to manager for approval (or auto-approved if within policy)
  5. Finance reconciliation and period close
  • Card transactions appear in the employee's queue within 24 hours of posting (real-time feed preferred, batch as fallback)
  • System auto-categorizes based on MCC and learns from corrections
  • Transactions sitting unreconciled for >5 days trigger a reminder to the employee
  • Unreconciled transactions at period close are flagged in finance dashboard with escalation options

Personal Card / Out-of-Pocket Flow

The exception path for personal card expenses. Higher friction by design to reinforce corporate card usage.

  1. Employee creates manual expense entry
  2. System prompts: "Do you have a corporate card?" with policy-level messaging
  3. If personal card allowed: justification required per policy level
  4. Receipt upload mandatory (no threshold exception for personal card)
  5. Routed to manager for approval
  6. Approved expenses added to reimbursement queue
  7. Finance processes reimbursement via payroll or direct payment
  • Personal card expenses always require a receipt regardless of amount
  • Justification for not using corporate card is a required field, visible in approval and audit views
  • Reimbursement is a separate payment process with its own configurable batch schedule
  • Dashboard prominently shows personal vs. corporate card ratio by employee and department

Mileage & Per-Diem

Mileage

  • IRS standard rate auto-updated annually (with manual override)
  • Google Maps integration for route calculation and distance verification
  • Round-trip and commute offset configurable by policy
  • Recurring route templates for frequent travelers

Per-Diem

  • GSA rates pre-loaded for US cities only
  • Company can override GSA with custom rates
  • Configurable as actuals-with-cap or flat allowance
  • Partial day calculations for departure/return days

Expense Reports

Expenses can be submitted individually or grouped into reports. Finance configures which modes are available.

Trip-Based Reports

Employee creates a trip container; all expenses for that trip are grouped together.

Period-Based Reports

Monthly submission of all outstanding expenses in a single batch.

Ad-Hoc Submission

Individual expense lines submitted without a report wrapper. Reduces month-end pileup.

Receipt Management

  • Mobile capture: Camera-based receipt scanning with OCR extraction (amount, date, vendor, tax)
  • Email forwarding: Dedicated receipt email address (receipts@[company].centime.com) that auto-attaches to matching card transaction
  • Bulk upload: Drag-and-drop multiple receipts with auto-matching
  • Match confidence: System shows match confidence score; employee confirms or corrects
  • Missing receipt affidavit: For lost receipts above threshold, employee signs a digital affidavit (configurable -- some companies block this entirely)
05 — Basic Purchasing

Lightweight pre-approved purchasing

This module handles pre-approved purchases that don't go through a formal procurement system. The goal is control and accountability, not full P2P.

Scope

In Scope Out of Scope
Office supplies and equipment Capital expenditures / fixed assets
Software subscriptions (<$X/year) Multi-year contracts
Professional services (consultants, contractors) RFP-driven sourcing
Conference and event registrations Inventory purchasing
Team meals and events Raw materials / manufacturing inputs

The dollar threshold for "basic" vs. "formal procurement" is company-configurable (e.g., purchases under $5,000).

Purchase Request Flow

  1. Employee creates purchase request with category, vendor, estimated amount, and business justification
  2. Policy engine validates: approval routing, card mandate
  3. Routed to manager (and optionally finance for amounts above threshold)
  4. Approved -- employee receives approval confirmation with PO number
  5. Employee makes purchase using corporate card
  6. Card transaction auto-matched to approved PO
  7. Three-way match: Request, Approval, Card Transaction
  8. Finance closes the loop

Purchase Request Fields

Field Required Notes
Category Yes Drives GL coding and approval routing
Vendor / Supplier Yes Free text or select from vendor list
Estimated Amount Yes Used for approval threshold routing
Business Justification Yes Why this purchase is needed
Department / Cost Center Yes Auto-populated from employee profile, editable
Project Code Configurable Required for project-based accounting
Recurring? Yes If yes: frequency and duration
Payment Method Auto Defaults to corporate card per policy

Purchase-to-Card Matching

  • System attempts auto-match based on vendor name, amount (within 10% tolerance), and date range (within 30 days of approval)
  • Matched transactions inherit GL coding and project codes from the approved request
  • Unmatched approved requests open for >30 days trigger a follow-up to the employee
  • Purchases made without a prior request are flagged as "unplanned spend" -- still processed, but visible in compliance reporting
06 — Approval Workflows

Configurable routing and actions

Approval routing is the link between policy enforcement and operational efficiency. The system supports multi-dimensional routing with configurable escalation.

Routing Rules

Amount Threshold

Different approvers at different dollar levels. E.g., <$500 = manager, $500-$5,000 = director, >$5,000 = VP + finance.

Category

Certain categories always route to specific approvers. E.g., travel to travel coordinator, software to IT.

Policy Violation

Any policy violation can add an additional approver. E.g., finance review for out-of-policy expenses.

Department

Department-specific approval chains with configurable levels and thresholds.

Approval Actions

Action Effect
Approve Moves to next approver in chain or to finance queue
Reject Returns to employee with required rejection reason (tied to policy where applicable)
Return for Correction Employee can edit and resubmit without starting over
Approve with Modification Approver can adjust GL code, cost center, or amount (with audit trail)

Auto-Approval

Expenses that meet ALL of the following criteria can be auto-approved (configurable):

  • Within policy limits
  • Corporate card transaction with receipt attached
  • Correct GL category (high-confidence auto-categorization)
  • Below auto-approval dollar threshold (company-configured, e.g., $100)
  • Employee has no active policy violations

Auto-approved expenses still appear in the manager's dashboard for visibility but do not require action.

Delegation & Escalation

  • Delegation: Approver can delegate to a named alternate for a date range (vacation coverage)
  • Escalation: Expenses pending approval for >X days auto-escalate to the next level in the chain
  • Batch approval: Manager can select multiple compliant expenses and approve in one action
07 — Finance Operations

Reconciliation, reimbursement, and GL coding

The finance workspace is the operational control center. It ties together card reconciliation, reimbursement processing, and general ledger management.

Corporate Card Reconciliation

The reconciliation workspace gives finance a single view of all card activity.

Status Meaning
Matched Card transaction matched to submitted, approved expense
Submitted -- Pending Employee submitted, waiting on manager approval
Unsubmitted Card transaction with no employee submission (aging tracked)
Disputed Employee flagged transaction as not theirs
Personal Charge Employee flagged as personal -- repayment tracked

Period Close Workflow

  1. Finance sets a submission deadline (e.g., 5th business day after month-end)
  2. System sends automated reminders at T-5, T-3, T-1, and T+0 days
  3. After deadline, unsubmitted transactions are escalated to employee's manager
  4. Finance can force-close with "unreconciled" flag for audit trail

Reimbursement Processing

For approved personal-card and out-of-pocket expenses.

  • Batch processing: Finance runs reimbursement batches on a configurable schedule
  • Payment methods: Direct deposit (ACH), payroll integration, or check
  • Approval: Finance approves the reimbursement batch before payment execution
  • Employee visibility: Employee sees status (approved, queued, processing, paid) with expected payment date

GL Coding & Allocation

  • Auto-coding: MCC-to-GL mapping with company-configurable rules
  • Split coding: Single expense allocated across multiple cost centers or projects
  • Default coding: Employee profile carries default department/cost center; overrideable per expense
  • Coding validation: Invalid combinations blocked at submission (e.g., project code X cannot be paired with department Y)
  • Period assignment: Expenses can be assigned to the period incurred vs. period submitted (accrual support)

Finance Dashboard & Reporting

Report Purpose
Policy Compliance Percentage of expenses in-policy by department, category, time period
Card Ratio Track adoption of corporate card mandate -- drill to department and individual
Unreconciled Aging Card transactions without employee submission, by age bucket
Spend by Category Spend by GL category, department, project with trend analysis
Approval Cycle Time Average time from submission to final approval
Top Spenders Highest spend employees/departments with trend lines
Duplicate Detection Flagged potential duplicate submissions
Reimbursement Queue Outstanding reimbursements by status and age
Audit Trail Export Full history of any expense: submission, edits, approvals, payment

09 — Mobile Experience

Mobile is not a companion app

Mobile must support the full employee and manager workflow. It is a primary interface, not an afterthought.

Employee Capabilities

  • Receipt capture with OCR
  • Quick-submit from card transaction notification
  • Mileage tracking (GPS-based)
  • View expense status and reimbursement timeline
  • Create purchase requests

Manager Capabilities

  • Review and approve/reject with swipe gestures
  • See policy flags and violation details
  • Batch approve compliant expenses
  • Delegate approval

Desktop only: Finance operations (reconciliation, reimbursement processing, GL management, reporting) are desktop/web workflows. Mobile is not required for finance operations.

10 — Security & Compliance

Access control, audit, and data protection

The platform must meet mid-market enterprise security standards while maintaining a complete audit trail for every action in the system.

  • Role-based access control: Employee, Manager, Finance Admin, Finance Viewer, System Admin
  • Audit trail: Every action logged -- submission, edit, approval, rejection, GL change, payment
  • Data retention: Configurable retention policies per document type (receipts, reports, approvals)
  • SOC 2 Type II: Required for mid-market enterprise sales
  • PCI compliance: No card numbers stored -- transaction data via tokenized feeds
  • Receipt storage: Encrypted at rest, access-controlled, tamper-evident

12 — Decisions & Open Questions

What we have decided, and what remains

All foundational product questions are now resolved except one. This section captures the locked decisions and the single remaining open question.

Decisions Locked

  • 1 Card program management -- RESOLVED: We are not an issuer. US Bank provides their own card management interface directly to clients for card issuance, limit management, and cardholder onboarding. We consume card transaction feeds only. FNBO is secondary/backup.
  • 2 International support -- RESOLVED: US only. No multi-currency, no international per-diem rates, no FX handling.
  • 3 Pricing model -- RESOLVED: Use our card (US Bank/FNBO) = free with committed spend. Use your own card = pay per active user per month.
  • 4 Build vs. partner -- RESOLVED: Built on Findity's white-label T&E platform. Findity provides the expense engine; we provide UX and workflows.
  • 5 No budget module -- RESOLVED: Centime Expense Management does not include budget tracking, budget checks, or actual-vs-budget reporting. Budgets live in the customer's ERP/GL. We push approved expense data; the ERP owns the budget comparison.
  • 6 Centime platform integration = GL/ERP only -- RESOLVED: The GL/ERP push is the connection to the broader Centime platform. No shared vendor master, no shared payment processing, no single unified dashboard, no bidirectional AP integration, no Centime payment rails for reimbursement.

Open Questions

  • 1 Findity integration architecture -- What is the boundary between our UX/workflow layer and Findity's engine? Where do we own the data model vs. consume theirs? How do we handle customization that goes beyond Findity's configuration surface? More to follow.
