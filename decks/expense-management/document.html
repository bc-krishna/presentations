Centime · Product Strategy

Expense Management Partnership Evaluation

A framework for selecting a card-agnostic, ERP-integrated expense management partner to serve Centime's mid-market clients.
February 2026 · Internal · Confidential
Platform Goal

Deliver a card-agnostic, ERP-integrated, partner-embeddable expense management platform that meets mid-market client needs — companies with $5M to $100M in revenue — with a core focus on expense capture, policy enforcement, and ERP synchronization across any card, any issuer.

01 — Strategic Context

Why this matters now

Centime's clients rely on QuickBooks, Sage Intacct, Microsoft Dynamics 365 Business Central, and Oracle NetSuite. Any expense management partner must meet them where they are.

Deep ERP Integration

Native connectors or open APIs for all four core ERPs.

Card Agnosticism

Multi-card, multi-issuer support — Visa, Mastercard, Amex, any bank.

Expense Automation

Receipt capture to approval to GL posting — best-in-class.

Embeddable

White-label ready for Centime and bank/channel partners.

03 — Must-Haves

Non-negotiable requirements

Any partner — regardless of origin or architecture — must deliver every capability on this list.

  • Card Transaction Feeds: Real-time or near-real-time from Visa, Mastercard, and Amex issuers.
  • Receipt Capture / OCR: Mobile upload, email ingestion, auto-matching to card transactions.
  • Approval Workflows: Multi-level, rule-based, cost-centre aware.
  • Policy Enforcement: Category rules, spend limits, duplicate checks.
  • Per-Diem / Mileage: IRS-compliant automation.
  • Reimbursement & AP Sync: Seamless link to Centime's AP module.
  • ERP Posting: Bi-directional sync to GL, dimensions, and projects.
  • Multi-Entity / Currency: Group and subsidiary support.
  • Analytics & Reporting: Spend insights and exportable data.
  • Mobile UX: Intuitive, white-label-ready apps.
  • Partner Enablement: APIs, SLA, and roadmap transparency.
04 — Gap-Closure Assessment

What each vendor needs to prove

Findity

  • Mature multi-issuer support; validate U.S. issuer coverage and feed speed
  • QuickBooks, NetSuite, Dynamics 365 listed; validate depth and Intacct plans
  • Add IRS per-diem and tax tables — low effort
  • Mature in EU; extend support coverage to U.S. hours
  • Proven enterprise volumes in EU

Vergo

  • Early stage; confirm issuer API availability and standardization
  • Build ERP connectors from scratch (estimated 6–9 months)
  • Already U.S. localized
  • Smaller team; SLA unknown
  • Needs mid-market load testing
05 — Evaluation Path Forward

Three phases to a decision

Phase 1 — Next 30 Days

Discovery

Technical deep dives with both vendors. Review API docs for card feeds and ERP connectors. Confirm U.S. ERP support across QB, NetSuite, Intacct, and Dynamics BC.

Phase 2 — Q1 2026

Pilot

Select one partner for a pilot with 3–5 U.S. clients across ERPs. Measure cycle time, posting accuracy, user satisfaction, and SLA response.

Phase 3 — Post-Pilot

Decision

Weighted scoring on ERP integration depth, card feed quality, functional completeness, SLA quality, and commercial scalability.

06 — Interim Recommendation

A dual-track approach while we evaluate

  • 01Keep Fyle for existing clients and short-term coverage.
  • 02Partner with Navan with a focus on NetSuite — especially important when clients need an integrated travel solution.
  • 03Evaluate Findity and Vergo in parallel, with Findity currently ahead on functional readiness — ERP connectivity and expense engine maturity.
  • 04Make ERP integration and card-feed maturity the defining evaluation criteria.
  • 05Delay bank-partner exposure until U.S. readiness is confirmed.
  • 06Focus proof-of-concept on ERP posting, multi-issuer card ingestion, and policy enforcement workflows.

Expense automation that works with any card and any ERP — no bank lock-in required. The platform that wins is the one that disappears into the client's existing stack.
