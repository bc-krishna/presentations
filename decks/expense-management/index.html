Expense Management Partnership Evaluation

A framework for selecting a card-agnostic, ERP-integrated expense partner for Centime's mid-market clients.
February 2026 · Internal
The Goal

One platform. Any card. Any ERP.

Centime needs an expense management partner that can serve mid-market companies — $5M to $100M in revenue — without forcing them to change their cards or their accounting system. The platform must handle expense capture, policy enforcement, and ERP synchronization across any issuer.

Non-Negotiable Requirements

Eleven capabilities every partner must deliver

  • Card Feeds: Real-time from Visa, Mastercard, Amex issuers.
  • Receipt / OCR: Mobile upload, email ingestion, auto-matching.
  • Approvals: Multi-level, rule-based, cost-centre aware.
  • Policy: Category rules, limits, duplicate checks.
  • Per-Diem / Mileage: IRS-compliant automation.
  • AP Sync: Seamless link to Centime's AP module.
  • ERP Posting: Bi-directional GL sync.
  • Multi-Entity: Group and subsidiary support.
  • Analytics: Spend insights and exports.
  • Mobile UX: White-label-ready apps.
  • Partner APIs: SLA and roadmap transparency.
Gap-Closure Assessment

What each vendor still needs to prove

Findity

Mature expense engine and multi-issuer support. Key gaps: validate U.S. issuer feed speed, confirm Sage Intacct plans, extend support to U.S. hours. IRS per-diem is low-effort. Proven at enterprise scale in EU.

Vergo

Strong U.S. presence and banking relationships. Key gaps: no ERP connectors — estimated 6–9 months to build. Issuer API maturity unclear. Smaller team, SLA unknown. Needs mid-market load testing.

Findity is closer to production readiness. Vergo's path is longer but its U.S. banking network is a strategic asset worth preserving.

Evaluation Path Forward

Three phases to a decision

Phase 1 — Next 30 Days

Technical deep dives with both vendors. Review API docs for card feeds and ERP connectors. Confirm U.S. ERP support for QB, NetSuite, Intacct, and Dynamics BC.

Phase 2 — Q1 2026

Select one partner for pilot. 3–5 U.S. clients across ERPs. Measure cycle time, posting accuracy, user satisfaction, and SLA response.

Phase 3 — Post-Pilot

Weighted scoring on ERP depth, card feed quality, functional completeness, SLA quality, and commercial scalability.

Interim Recommendation

A dual-track approach while we evaluate

  • 01Keep Fyle for existing clients and short-term coverage.
  • 02Partner with Navan for NetSuite — especially when clients need integrated travel.
  • 03Evaluate Findity and Vergo in parallel — Findity currently ahead on ERP connectivity and expense engine maturity.
  • 04Make ERP integration and card-feed maturity the defining evaluation criteria.
  • 05Delay bank-partner exposure until U.S. readiness is confirmed.
  • 06Focus proof-of-concept on ERP posting, multi-issuer card ingestion, and policy enforcement.

Expense automation that works with any card and any ERP — no bank lock-in required. The platform that wins is the one that disappears into the client's existing stack.
