Centime · Product Specification

Expense Management

A policy-driven expense platform built on Findity's white-label framework for US mid-market finance teams. Card-agnostic, but designed to favor our cards. UX and workflows first.
v0.2 Draft · February 2026 · BC / Product · US Market · US Bank (Primary) · FNBO (Backup)
What We Believe

Four principles that shape every decision

Principle 01

Our Card First, Any Card Accepted

We issue T&E cards through US Bank (primary) and FNBO (backup). Every UX nudge and workflow favors our card. But the system is card-agnostic — employees using their own cards pay per active user per month; our card is free with committed spend.

Principle 02

Policy at the Center

Rules are not bolted on after the fact. They shape the interface the employee sees, the options available to them, and the approval routing that follows.

Principle 03

Built on Findity, Not from Scratch

Findity's white-label T&E platform is purpose-built for this. We bring the UX, workflows, card partnerships, and Centime integration. They bring the expense engine. Mid-market pragmatism: 200 to 2,000 employees without enterprise configuration overhead.

Principle 04

Accountability Without Friction

Capture what finance needs for audit and GL coding without making employees hate the process. The system should feel helpful, not punitive.

Who We're Building For

Three personas, three sets of needs

Employee

The submitter. Road warriors, occasional travelers, department leads making purchases. They need to submit expenses quickly with minimal data entry, understand what policy covers before spending, and know the status of every pending reimbursement. Corporate card transactions should flow in automatically so they never retype what the bank already knows.

"I don't know what I'm allowed to spend, and I submitted two weeks ago with no idea where it is."

Manager

The approver. Direct managers, department heads, project leads in the approval chain. They need to review and approve from mobile, see policy violations flagged without reading every line item, and delegate authority when out of office.

"I get a pile of expense reports at month-end and can't tell which ones actually need my attention."

Finance

The controller and reimburser. AP team, finance managers, controllers who close the books and reconcile cards. They need consistent policy enforcement, clean card reconciliation, proper GL coding, audit trails, and visibility into spend patterns and compliance rates across the organization.

"Half the card transactions are unmatched and I spend hours fixing GL codes employees picked wrong."

The Backbone

The policy engine shapes everything

Policies are not validation rules bolted onto a form. They are the system's central nervous system, shaping what employees see, what options are available, how approvals route, and how violations are handled. The engine evaluates a layered hierarchy where specificity wins:

Company Default — the baseline rules every employee inherits
Department / Division Override — sales may have different meal limits than engineering
Role-Based Override — executives or field sales get expanded thresholds
Project / Event Override — temporary rules for a conference or client engagement

Employees should never have to guess. The system surfaces applicable policies before a trip, during submission with real-time validation, and in rejection messages tied to specific named rules rather than generic manager feedback. Violations are handled with three configurable responses: soft warnings the employee can override, hard warnings requiring justification, and hard blocks that prevent submission entirely.

Enforcing the Default

Four levels of corporate card mandate

The system provides graduated enforcement for our Centime-issued cards (US Bank / FNBO). Each expense category can be assigned its own level. Employees using their own cards are always accepted but pay per active user per month. The recommended default is Level 2 company-wide, escalating to Level 3 for T&E categories above $50.

Level 1 · Encouraged

Gentle Reminder

Personal card is accepted. The employee sees a reminder that corporate card is preferred, but no justification is required and no friction is added to the submission.

Level 2 · Preferred

Justify the Exception

Personal card is accepted but requires a written justification: forgot card, vendor didn't accept, etc. The justification is visible to both the approver and finance for review.

Level 3 · Required

Block with Exception Path

Personal card submission is blocked for this category. The employee must use their corporate card or submit a policy exception request that routes to finance for pre-approval.

Level 4 · Mandatory

Auto-Reject

Personal card expenses in this category are automatically rejected with a message directing the employee to use their corporate card. No exception path without a finance override.

T&E Expenses

The core flow: card to reconciliation

The primary path begins when a corporate card transaction posts. The system auto-matches to a merchant category code, pre-populates an expense line, and drops it into the employee's queue within 24 hours. The employee reviews, attaches a receipt, confirms the GL and project code, and submits. If the expense is within policy, below the auto-approval threshold, and has a receipt attached, it can be auto-approved and flow straight to finance reconciliation.

1. Card transaction posts and auto-categorizes via MCC
2. Pre-populated expense appears in employee queue
3. Employee reviews, attaches receipt, confirms coding
4. Auto-approved if in-policy, or routed to manager
5. Finance reconciles card statement against approved expenses

The personal card exception path always requires a receipt regardless of amount, justification for not using the corporate card, and routes through the standard approval chain before entering the reimbursement queue. Expenses can be submitted individually in real-time, grouped into trip-based reports, or batched monthly.

Capture & Compliance

Receipts, mileage, and per-diem

Mobile Capture

Camera-Based OCR

Point the phone at a receipt and the system extracts amount, date, vendor, and tax. Match confidence scores let the employee confirm or correct before submission.

Email Forwarding

Inbox to Expense

A dedicated receipts@ address auto-attaches forwarded receipts to matching card transactions. Bulk drag-and-drop upload is available on desktop for batch reconciliation.

Mileage

IRS Rates + GPS

IRS standard rate auto-updated annually with manual override. Google Maps integration for route calculation and distance verification. Round-trip and commute offsets configurable by policy, with recurring templates for frequent routes.

Per-Diem

GSA Rates Built In

GSA rates pre-loaded for US cities. Companies can override with custom rates. Supports actuals-with-cap or flat allowance modes, including partial-day calculations for departure and return days. US only at launch.

Lightweight P2P

Basic purchasing: control without overhead

This module handles pre-approved purchases that do not go through a formal procurement system. It covers office supplies, software subscriptions, professional services, conference registrations, and team events below a company-configurable threshold. Capital expenditures, multi-year contracts, and inventory purchasing are explicitly out of scope. The goal is control and accountability, not full procure-to-pay.

1. Employee creates a purchase request with category, vendor, amount, and justification
2. Policy engine validates and routes to the right approver
3. Approved request generates a PO number sent to the employee
4. Employee makes the purchase on corporate card
5. Card transaction auto-matched to approved PO within 10% amount and 30-day window
6. Three-way match closes the loop: request, approval, card transaction

Purchases made without a prior request are flagged as unplanned spend. They still process through the system, but they surface in compliance reporting so finance can see patterns and address them.

Workflow Engine

Approval routing that adapts to context

Approval routing is configurable by amount threshold, expense category, policy violation status, and department. A $200 meal routes to the direct manager. A $3,000 software purchase routes to the director and then finance. Any policy violation can add an extra approver to the chain automatically.

Core Actions

Approve, Reject, Return, Modify

Approvers can approve, reject with a policy-linked reason, return for correction without restarting the process, or approve with a modified GL code or cost center while preserving the full audit trail.

Auto-Approval

When Everything Is Clean

Expenses within policy, on corporate card with receipt attached, correctly categorized, below the auto-approval threshold, and from an employee with no active violations can skip the queue. They still appear in the manager's dashboard for visibility.

Delegation

Vacation Coverage

An approver delegates to a named alternate for a defined date range. When they return, authority reverts automatically. No approvals should sit orphaned because someone is out of office.

Escalation

Aging Protection

Expenses pending approval beyond a configurable number of days auto-escalate to the next level in the chain. Batch approval lets managers select multiple compliant expenses and approve in a single action.

Finance Workspace

Reconciliation, reimbursement, and GL coding

The reconciliation workspace gives finance a single view of every corporate card transaction: matched to an approved expense, submitted and pending approval, unsubmitted and aging, disputed by the employee, or flagged as a personal charge with repayment tracked. At period close, the system sends automated reminders at T-5, T-3, T-1, and deadline, then escalates unsubmitted transactions to managers.

Reimbursement

Approved personal-card expenses enter a reimbursement queue. Finance runs batches on a configurable schedule, approves the batch, and pays via ACH, payroll integration, or check. Employees see real-time status from approved through processing to paid, with an expected payment date.

GL Coding

MCC-to-GL auto-mapping with company-configurable rules. Single expenses can be split across multiple cost centers or projects. Invalid GL combinations are blocked at submission. Period assignment supports accrual accounting by separating the period incurred from the period submitted.

Reporting

Policy compliance scorecards, corporate-vs-personal card ratios, unreconciled aging by bucket, spend by category against budget, approval cycle time, top spenders with trend lines, duplicate detection, and full audit trail exports. Everything finance needs to close the books.

System Connections

Integrations: required and desired

The platform is built on Findity's white-label T&E engine. Around it, we integrate card feeds, employee data, and the general ledger. The GL/ERP integration is the connection point to the broader Centime platform — nothing more is needed.

Required

Findity Platform — White-label T&E engine providing the core expense processing, receipt OCR, and policy framework. We build the UX and workflows on top.
Visa & Mastercard Feeds — Real-time transaction feeds from our US Bank and FNBO card programs. Tokenized for PCI compliance. No real-time Amex integration.
ERP / GL — The connection point to the broader Centime platform. QuickBooks, NetSuite, Sage Intacct, Microsoft Dynamics. Outbound push of approved expense data with full GL coding.
HRIS — BambooHR, ADP, Workday. Inbound sync of employee data, department, manager, and cost center assignments.
SSO / Directory — Okta, Azure AD, Google Workspace. Authentication and automated user provisioning.

Desired

Calendar — Google Calendar, Outlook. Associate expenses with meetings and events for automatic business purpose documentation.
Slack / Teams — Approval notifications and quick-approve actions so managers never leave their communication tool to clear a queue.
Bank Account Verification — CAS integration to verify employee bank accounts for ACH reimbursement, reducing payment errors and fraud risk.
Travel Booking — Deprioritized. We will not compete with Navan on travel management. Basic trip container support is a future consideration.
Platform Requirements

Mobile-native and secure by design

Employee Mobile

Full Workflow, Not a Companion

Receipt capture with OCR, quick-submit from card transaction notifications, GPS-based mileage tracking, expense status and reimbursement timeline views, and purchase request creation. Everything an employee needs, from their phone.

Manager Mobile

Approve from Anywhere

Review and approve or reject with swipe gestures. See policy flags and violation details at a glance. Batch approve compliant expenses and delegate approval authority. Finance operations remain desktop-only by design.

Access Control

Five Roles, Clear Boundaries

Role-based access across Employee, Manager, Finance Admin, Finance Viewer, and System Admin. Every action logged with a tamper-evident audit trail covering submissions, edits, approvals, rejections, GL changes, and payments.

Compliance

SOC 2 and PCI

SOC 2 Type II required for mid-market enterprise sales. PCI compliance maintained by never storing card numbers — transaction data arrives via tokenized feeds. Receipts encrypted at rest with access-controlled, tamper-evident storage. Configurable retention policies per document type.

Rollout Plan

Four phases from MVP to full platform

Built on Findity's white-label platform, our first objective is to deliver the UX and workflows. We ship the core expense workflow first on the Findity engine, then expand outward. Travel management is explicitly deprioritized — we will not compete with Navan.

Phase 1 · MVP

UX and workflows on Findity. Expense submission, Visa/MC real-time feeds from US Bank, basic policies, and approval workflows. The core loop from card swipe to finance reconciliation.

Phase 2 · +4 Weeks

Card-agnostic personal card flow, reimbursement processing, the advanced policy engine with full hierarchy, and mobile app for employees and managers.

Phase 3 · +8 Weeks

Basic purchasing module with purchase-to-card matching, three-way match, and unplanned spend flagging.

Phase 4 · +12 Weeks

Advanced reporting and dashboards, FNBO backup card program integration, expanded ERP connectors.
